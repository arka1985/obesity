Obesity Risk Assessment Tool for Adult Indian

Based on BMI, Waist Circumference, and Clinical Manifestations

Enter Your Details

BMI Classification

  • Normal: 18.5 – 22.9
  • Overweight: 23.0 – 24.9
  • Obesity (Class I): 25.0 – 29.9
  • Obesity (Class II): ≥ 30.0

Waist Circumference

  • Normal: ≤ 90 cm (Men), ≤ 80 cm (Women)
  • High Risk: > 90 cm (Men), > 80 cm (Women)

Clinical Manifestations of obesity-related organ dysfunction or alterations of daily activities

  • CNS: Signs of raised intracranial pressure such as vision loss and/or recurrent headaches
  • Upper airways: Apnoeas/hypopnoeas during sleep due to increased upper airways resistance
  • Respiratory: Hypoventilation, breathlessness, or wheezing due to reduced lung/diaphragm compliance
  • Cardiovascular (ventricular): Reduced LV systolic function - HFrEF (Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction)
  • Cardiovascular (atrial): Chronic/recurrent atrial fibrillation
  • Cardiovascular (pulmonary): Pulmonary artery hypertension
  • Cardiovascular: Chronic fatigue, lower limb edema due to HFpEF (Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction)
  • Cardiovascular (thrombosis): Recurrent DVT and/or pulmonary embolism
  • Cardiovascular (arterial): Raised arterial blood pressure
  • Metabolism: Hyperglycaemia, high triglycerides, and low HDL cholesterol
  • Liver: NAFLD with hepatic fibrosis
  • Renal: Microalbuminuria with reduced eGFR
  • Urinary: Recurrent or chronic urinary incontinence
  • Reproductive (female): Anovulation, oligo-menorrhea, and PCOS
  • Reproductive (male): Male hypogonadism
  • Musculoskeletal: Severe knee/hip pain with stiffness and reduced motion
  • Lymphatic: Lower limb lymphedema causing pain and reduced motion
  • Limitations: Significant age-adjusted limitations of mobility or ADLs
